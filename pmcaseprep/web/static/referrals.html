🤝 Referral Paths

find the people who'd actually refer you

A referral multiplies your interview odds — and you already know the people. This tool cross-references your own data exports (LinkedIn, phone contacts, Instagram, Facebook) into one relationship-strength map: who's actually close, who's already talked jobs with you, and who works where you're applying. Then, with pods, you and your friends pool who-can-refer-where — without anyone's contact list leaving their device.

🔒 Solo mode is 100% local. Every file is parsed in your browser — names, companies, emails are never uploaded. The only exception is the opt-in pods feature below, which shares exactly two things: company names and irreversible hashes — never names. Each step says exactly what leaves the page.

1 · Get your exports, drop them all here

Every platform is legally required to give you your data. APIs are dead; the export files are the one ToS-clean door — and they keep your data in your hands. Start with LinkedIn (it's the spine); each extra source sharpens the closeness ranking.

💼 LinkedIn — the full archive (start here)
  1. LinkedIn → Me → Settings & Privacy → Data privacy → Get a copy of your data
  2. Choose “Download larger data archive” — it includes connections, messages, education, positions, recommendations and invitations (all of which power the ranking below)
  3. LinkedIn emails a first archive in ~10 minutes (it has most files) and the complete one within 24 h — either works
  4. Drop the whole .zip below — no need to unzip

In a hurry? “Want something in particular?” → tick Connections + Messages + Invitations + Profile + Recommendations — arrives in minutes. A bare Connections.csv alone works too (that's the v1 flow).

📱 Phone contacts — your closest people (WhatsApp proxy)
  1. iPhone: Contacts app → Lists (top-left) → touch & hold All Contacts → Export → save the .vcf — or iCloud.com → Contacts → select all → Export vCard
  2. Android: Contacts app → Fix & manage → Export to file (.vcf)
  3. Google Contacts: contacts.google.com → Export → vCard or Google CSV
  4. Drop the .vcf (or Google .csv) below

Your phonebook ≈ your WhatsApp graph — the close-friends layer LinkedIn misses entirely. Someone in both your phone AND your LinkedIn is a real relationship.

📸 Instagram — close friends list + DMs
  1. Instagram → profile → menu → Settings → Accounts Center → Your information and permissions → Download your information
  2. Download or transfer information → pick your Instagram account → Some of your information → tick Followers and following + Messages
  3. Download to device · date range All time · format JSON (important — not HTML) · media quality Low
  4. The link lands by email (minutes to a few hours) → drop the .zip below

Your hand-picked Close Friends list and the people you actually DM are gold-grade closeness signals — both are in this export.

👥 Facebook — friends list + Messenger
  1. Same flow: Accounts Center → Download your information → pick your Facebook profile
  2. Tick Friends and followers + Messages → format JSON → All time
  3. Drop the .zip below

The friends-list API died in 2015 — but your own export has the full list, with real names that match against your LinkedIn.

Drop everything here — LinkedIn .zip, Connections.csv, contacts .vcf, Instagram/Facebook .zip — or click to choose. Add files in any order, any time.

4 · Job-hunt pods — multiply with friends

Job hunting works better as a squad. A pod pools two things across friends: who can refer directly where (everyone's work history becomes a referral exchange) and who knows people at which companies — your 2nd-degree network, unlocked through people who'd actually vouch for you.

🔒 What leaves your browser if you share into a pod (and only then): company names from your work history, and one row per connection — SHA-256 hash of their profile URL + their company name. No names, no emails, no titles — the server rejects anything else by construction. Pod-mates see counts (“Rahul knows 3 people at Stripe”), and only Rahul's own browser can resolve which 3, because only he holds his export. Leave a pod and your rows are deleted.
